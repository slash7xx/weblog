What IS CRISPR?

CRISPR is an incrediibly useful genetic editing technology that's used for a multitude of reasons. But what is it? well, to start us off, RNA, or ribonucleic acid, is like a tiny, less permanent DNA. It comes in strands, not double helixes and a strand has to be created to locate the gene that needs to be edited. It's incredibly necessary that the RNA is identical to whatever gene you're trying to find as none of the steps after this will work. Next, an enzyme called Cas9 merges with the RNA to create the RNP complex, which is what does all the fun stuff. The Cas9 then "cuts" the DNA into two strands, and after that you can cut, delete, or replace DNA.
